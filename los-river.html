Once the greatest source of fresh water in the Los Angeles Basin the LA river is a metamorphosed construction of manmade revisualization of nature into concrete. When I lived in Japan I noticed it in all of the rivers, they called it Japanification, well I see just who they learned it from. Perhaps we should have called it Americanzaion. Following catastrophic flooding of the late 1930's and part of the Works Progress Administration and constructed by the Army corp of engineers the massive concrete sub structure cuts through the city like concrete flow of lava. Used for numerous movie car chases and featuring the iconic 4th and 6th street bridges which have also played as a key Hollywood backdrops. Now with changing ideologies toward our impact on reconstructing nature; a LA river 'deconstruction' and return to natural settings is underway. Furthermore, iron cancer has struck at the heart of the iconic bridges and their demolition was set to begin in the weeks following the shooting of these photos.
